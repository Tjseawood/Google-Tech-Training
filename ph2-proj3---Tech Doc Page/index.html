Introduction

The major scale is one of the most commonly used musical scales, especially in Western Music. Like many musical scales, it is made up of seven notes: the eighth duplicates the first at double its frequency so that it is called a higher octave of the same note.In this document we will discuss how to build major scale and where they are found in modern western culture.

Formula

Interval

Before attempted to build a major scale, we music start with defining an interval. An interval in music is defined as a distance in pitch between any two notes. Is it best to think of intervals as equvilent to letters in the alphabet, they can be strung together to create larger structures; similar to words and sentences.

There are a few intervals but none are most important than the minor 2nd and the major 2nd, also known as a half-step and a whole-step. For similiply, we will reference these 2 intervals as half and whole step for the duration of the document.

  • C to D = whole-step
  • C to C sharp (D flat) = half-step

How to build a major scale

There are seven different notes in each scale. Scale degree refers to the position of each scale note. Each scale note has a scale degree name. For example, C is the first scale degree of C major scale.

We start by selecting our inital note (a.k.a the root or tonic) them we follow a specific intervalic sequence:

  • Start at the Root
  • then ascend up a whole-step
  • followed by another whole-step
  • then a half-step
  • follow up with another whole-step
  • then another whole-step
  • one more whole-step
  • Finally finish with a half-step
This formula is absolute and will always produce a major scale, regardless of the starting pitch. Here is another way that it is commony written. R W-W-H-W-W-W-H

Major scale examples:

Here is a major scale build from starting on the note C: C D E F G A B C Here is another starting on G: G A B C D E F# G Here is one more starting on the note B: B C# D# E F# G# A# B Although they have their difference in appearence, they all follow the exact same formula for building a major scale and thus feel the same when heared.
